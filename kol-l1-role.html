😍 Level 1 · Why Energy Now

I'm a KOL… What role can I play in the Energy Transition?

You already have influence. The question is: what do you do with it? This page helps you understand your unique power — and the specific ways you can use it to move the climate conversation forward.

Content Creators vs. KOLs

The terms content creator and KOL are often used interchangeably. Both influence audiences through what they share online. But they come from different starting points and have different strengths. Neither is more important — they simply work in different ways.

On this page, we use the terms interchangeably to refer to anyone who creates influence through content.

✍️
Content Creators
What they do: Make and share digital content — videos, photos, blogs, podcasts, social media posts.
Core skill: Storytelling & audience engagement.
Influence comes from: Relatability and visibility.
Leverage: A direct emotional link with followers. They know what their audience wants and how to make it appealing.
🎓
KOLs (Key Opinion Leaders)
What they do: Share expertise within their field — dermatologists, architects, nutritionists.
Core strength: Credibility & authority.
Influence comes from: Knowledge and authenticity.
Leverage: Expert trust built over time. They only share what they truly believe in.

Whether you're a content creator or a KOL, you are an influential person. When people see your stories, videos, or posts, they begin to want something they never thought about before. And what you say can make them believe they need it. Your opinion carries power. It shapes how people think, feel, and act.

"Most of us don't realize it, but consumers hold incredible power. 🔥 Every peso we spend sends a message about the kind of culture we want to live in. Too often, that power gets wasted on mindless flexes — hauls, logos, status symbols."

— @basicallyborrowed

Two Types of KOL Superpowers

Every influential person has a natural mode. Which one feels most like you?

📣
The Megaphone — Reach

You're the voice that carries across the crowd. Your posts fly far, fast, and wide. You set the vibe for the masses — sometimes reaching people you've never even met. Think of it like tossing a stone into a lake: your ripples touch far-off shores.

⚡️ Your strength is scale. You make climate action hard to ignore.

Real examples from The Good Chat

Buji Babiera @bujibabiera · 3.7M followers on TikTok 🇵🇭
›
Who is he?

Buji Babiera is a Filipino content creator and soft mobility advocate, encouraging Filipinos to embrace cycling as an eco-friendly alternative to cars. Once a car enthusiast himself, Buji's personal transformation into a passionate cyclist inspired thousands to rethink urban mobility, community connection, and climate action through everyday choices.

Why he joined The Good Chat

Buji joined to expand his impact beyond mobility and explore how digital storytelling could drive broader conversations around the energy transition — connecting sustainable transport, renewable energy, and climate resilience.

What he achieved

Buji began sharing insights from visits to solar farms across the Philippines, sparking pride and curiosity. His most powerful moment came when he returned to Tacloban, devastated by Typhoon Yolanda in 2013. His video on the Philippines' vulnerability to climate change generated 4.6 million views on TikTok and nearly 300,000 reactions — turning grief and anger into awareness and action.

Who is she?

Maria Lynn Ehren is a Thai public figure, model, and advocate crowned Miss Universe Thailand 2017. Since 2020, she has used her platform to raise awareness on animal welfare, sustainability, and wellbeing.

Why she joined The Good Chat

Maria wanted to go beyond advocacy and take concrete action for the environment. Through the fellowship, she deepened her understanding of Thailand's energy challenges and conducted audience polls to assess public attitudes toward renewable energy.

What she achieved

Driven by her newfound passion for renewable energy, Maria launched her own solar panel company and foundation, helping Thai SMEs access clean energy solutions. She also began collaborating with Greenpeace Thailand on campaigns against fossil fuels.

The Campfire — Engagement

You gather people close and spark conversations that feel safe and relatable. You listen as much as you talk, so people trust you deeply. Around your "campfire," followers feel like part of a community, not just an audience.

⚡️ Your strength is connection. You make climate action feel personal.

Real examples from The Good Chat

Puty Puar @byputy · Founder of Buibu Baca Buku (90,000 members) 🇮🇩

Who is she?

Puty Puar is an Indonesian illustrator, writer, and community leader. She founded Buibu Baca Buku, a women's book club with over 90,000 members across Indonesia, where mothers and young women exchange ideas on parenting, education, and personal growth.

Why she joined The Good Chat

Puty joined to explore how motherhood could become an entry point for conversations about the climate crisis — connecting health, safety, and children's futures to pollution, energy transition, and sustainability.

Frances Bautista Founder of Home Buddies · 3M members on Facebook 🇵🇭

Who is she?

Frances Bautista is the founder of Home Buddies, one of the Philippines' largest online communities with over 3 million members — originally created for home improvement tips and lifestyle, now a powerful digital forum for everyday Filipinos.

Why she joined The Good Chat

Frances was interested in how everyday concerns — like rising electricity bills — could serve as relatable entry points to talk about cleaner, more affordable energy solutions.

What she achieved

Frances launched a conversation about soaring electricity costs in the Philippines. The thread went viral — thousands shared their struggles, which led to an organic wave of interest in solar energy, with members sharing photos of their home solar installations. She then produced a documentary-style video series visiting solar homeowners, showing that renewable energy was an accessible, community-driven solution.

Megaphone energy makes the general public and influential people pay attention.
Campfire energy makes the general public take action.

Whether you reach wide or engage deep — your spark keeps the climate fire burning.

How KOLs Can Drive the Energy Transition

Two panel discussions with experts and community leaders from Thailand and Indonesia.

⚠️ You don't need to know everything about energy policy to make a difference.

Leave the heavy policy lifting to the civil society organizations (CSOs) who work on it every day. Your role is different — and just as powerful. You make the transition visible, wanted, and urgent.

Every conversation you start, every post you share, every livestream you host adds up to social proof. It shows people that others care — and that caring about climate is normal, not niche. Decision-makers are paying attention. When they see public figures like you speaking out, they feel both the pressure and the legitimacy to act.
