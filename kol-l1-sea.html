🌏 Level 1 · Why Energy Now

Climate Change & the Energy Transition in Southeast Asia: what's really at stake?

What's at stake in Southeast Asia's energy transition is nothing less than our health, livelihoods, and future resilience in a warming world.

🔥
Ouf, it's getting hotter and wetter every day.

Climate change is already reshaping our daily lives — from struggling to get to work during stronger and more frequent storms, to avoiding playing outside with our kids because of the heat and air pollution, to paying higher and higher electricity bills because of increased aircon consumption.

But it doesn't have to stay this way.

We can do something about it — and one of the biggest solutions is speeding up the energy transition: shifting from polluting fossil fuels to clean, affordable, and renewable energy for everyone.

Let's be honest — talking about energy isn't exactly "sexy."

But changing the way we produce and use energy is one of the most important things we can do for the future of our people and our countries.

An energy transition grounded in renewables doesn't just tackle the climate crisis — it brings real, everyday benefits. According to IRENA ↗, switching to clean energy can:

🌱
Create new jobs and boost local economies
🌏
Drive sustainable development that benefits communities, not just corporations
💨
Improve public health by reducing air pollution and exposure to natural disasters
☀️
Reduce heat waves and make our homes more resilient to rising temperatures
🥦
Protect our favorite foods and products — from chocolate and beer to vegetables and meat

The energy transition isn't just about saving the planet. It's about making sure life stays livable, affordable, and fair for everyone.
